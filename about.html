About Me

Hey there! I'm Jacob, and I've spent the last six years diving deep into Missouri's legislative process as a Legislative Coordinator with the state's Budget & Planning division. What started as a Political Science degree from Truman State turned into a career I'm genuinely passionate about - helping make government work better for everyone.

My days involve coordinating a team of twelve analysts, managing the flow of legislative information between state agencies and the Governor's Office, and making sure complex policy proposals get the thorough review they deserve. I'm particularly proud of helping Missouri modernize our fiscal note review process - we shifted from outdated systems to a streamlined web-based platform that's made everyone's job easier.

I'm a problem-solver at heart who thrives on turning complicated policy challenges into clear, actionable insights. Whether I'm presenting to state leadership or breaking down legislation for my team, I believe good communication is the key to effective governance. I've even shared insights with NASBO (National Association of State Budget Officers) on "Smart Brevity" - a communication technique that helps state budget analysts engage audiences more effectively when messaging to government officials. When I'm not immersed in the world of bills and budget analysis, you'll find me exploring Missouri's beautiful trails with friends - there's nothing like a good hike to clear your head after a long session at the Capitol!

Recent Reads

Books that have shaped my thinking lately

The Notebook: A History of Thinking on Paper

Roland Allen

Fascinating exploration of how notebooks revolutionized creative thinking. As someone who reviews countless documents, I found the parallels between historical note-taking and modern policy analysis incredibly relevant.

Nexus: A Brief History of Information Networks

Yuval Noah Harari

Harari's latest masterpiece on information networks from ancient times to AI. Essential reading for understanding how information shapes power structures - directly applicable to legislative processes and government communications.

Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI

Ethan Mollick

Practical insights on working alongside AI tools. As government modernizes its processes, understanding how to leverage AI while maintaining human oversight is crucial for policy analysis and public service.

The Ministry for the Future

Kim Stanley Robinson

Compelling climate fiction that explores future governance structures. Robinson's vision of institutional innovation offers thought-provoking perspectives on how government might adapt to address long-term challenges.
