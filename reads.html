Books that have shaped my thinking lately

The Notebook: A History of Thinking on Paper

Roland Allen

Fascinating exploration of how notebooks revolutionized creative thinking. As someone who reviews countless documents, I found the parallels between historical note-taking and modern policy analysis incredibly relevant.

Nexus: A Brief History of Information Networks

Yuval Noah Harari

Harari's latest masterpiece on information networks from ancient times to AI. Essential reading for understanding how information shapes power structures - directly applicable to legislative processes and government communications.

Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI

Ethan Mollick

Practical insights on working alongside AI tools. As government modernizes its processes, understanding how to leverage AI while maintaining human oversight is crucial for policy analysis and public service. See my Claude Model Guide for tracking the latest releases.

The Ministry for the Future

Kim Stanley Robinson

Compelling climate fiction that explores future governance structures. Robinson's vision of institutional innovation offers thought-provoking perspectives on how government might adapt to address long-term challenges.
